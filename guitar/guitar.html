This page is meant to be practiced 5 minutes a day. If you do more, you won’t learn as much. You don’t need to know music theory at all — just the concept of an interval: the distance between two notes.

Recognize Sound of Intervals (6 months)

There are 13 total intervals. Use a ✅ mnemonic table to memorize them. For example, the interval of a major second appears in the "…ppy birth…" part of "Happy Birthday". Focus on the lyrics.

I memorized intervals by listening to just the song fragment with the interval using audio mass app. It took me two weeks per interval with just 5 minutes a day.

While learning, test yourself with the intervals you know on the Toned Ear website . When you consistently score 90% accuracy, move to the fretboard section.

Recognize Interval on Fretboard (2 weeks)

Now that you can identify the intervals with your ear, it's time to learn ✅ their position on the guitar fretboard. Start with the last 4 strings (G, D, A, E). These are the most common shapes used.

Practice 5 minutes a day using this app.

Recognizing Interval Sound and Position (1 year)

Progress checklist:

  • ✅ Sing one note
  • ✅ Sing two notes in a row
  • ✅ Sing three notes in a row
  • ✅ More than three notes

Can hear and play:

  • ✅ Two notes
  • ✅ Three notes
  • ✅ More than three notes

Can sing over chord progressions:

  • ✅ One note
  • ✅ Two notes
  • ✅ Three notes
  • ✅ More than three notes

Note: Practice at 70 BPM. Faster BPM is harder.

Thinking then playing (gain precision):

  • ✅ Make words
  • ✅ Think at least three notes then play them
  • ✅ Make phrases
  • ✅ Join notes into meaningful phrases

Thinking and playing immediately (exchange precision for speed):

  • ✅ Play in real time (≤1s per note)
