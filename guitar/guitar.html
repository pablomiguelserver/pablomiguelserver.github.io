Guitar

This page is meant to be practiced with two sets of 5 minutes (one in the morning and one in the night) or only five minutes a day(you will get there either way).If you do more, you won’t learn as much. You don’t need to know music theory at all — just the concept of an interval: the distance between two notes. everything else will be taught in this webpage.

Recognize Sound of Intervals (6 months)

It is important to know which interval is being played, and whether it is ascending or descending. We will use mnemonics—a common way of learning them. There are 13 total intervals. Use a ✅ mnemonic table to memorize them.

Recognize Interval on Fretboard (2 weeks)

In this stage, you’ll focus on not just hearing intervals but also locating ✅ their position on the guitar fretboard. Every interval has a unique shape or pattern on the fretboard

Recognizing Interval Sound and Position (1 year)

We need to understand a loop. First you need to recognize the interval by ear, find it in the fretboard and then play the interval. For every loop, you start again where you left off.

Progress checklist:

Can sing and play:

  • ✅ One interval (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Two intervals (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Three intervals (2 weeks)
  • ✅ More than three intervals (2 weeks)

Can hear and play (use this dictation app):

  • ✅ Two intervals (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Three intervals (2 weeks)
  • ✅ More than three intervals (2 weeks)

Can sing over chord progressions:(use the app chordchord)

  • ✅ One interval (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Two intervals (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Three intervals (2 weeks)
  • ✅ More than three intervals (2 weeks)

Note: Practice at 70 BPM. Faster BPM is harder.

Thinking then playing (gain precision):

  • ✅ Make words (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Think at least three notes then play them (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Make phrases (2 weeks)
  • ✅ Join notes into meaningful phrases (2 weeks)

Thinking and playing immediately (exchange precision for speed):

  • ✅ Play in real time (≤1s per note)
